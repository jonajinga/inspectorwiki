# Fire & Safety Overview

Fire and life safety inspection covers smoke and CO alarms, fire stopping, egress, and safety glazing. These are among the most important findings for occupant safety.

## Smoke and CO Alarms

- Smoke alarms required on every level, inside every bedroom, and outside sleeping areas
- CO alarms required on every level with fuel-burning appliances or attached garages
- Units older than 10 years should be replaced regardless of function

## Egress

- Every bedroom must have an emergency escape opening (window or door)
- Minimum clear opening: 5.7 sq ft (5.0 sq ft at grade), 20" wide, 24" high
- Maximum sill height: 44" above floor

## Fire Stopping

- Gaps around pipes, wires, and ducts passing through fire-rated assemblies must be sealed
- Garage-to-house separation requires fire-rated drywall and a self-closing door

:::danger
Missing or non-functional smoke alarms are an immediate safety concern and should always be reported as a priority item.
:::
