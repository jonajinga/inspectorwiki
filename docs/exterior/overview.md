# Exterior & Siding Overview

The exterior inspection covers all visible surfaces and site conditions, including siding, trim, grading, drainage, walkways, and driveways.

## Siding Types

- **Vinyl** — check for buckling, gaps at joints, and ground clearance (6" minimum)
- **Wood** — look for rot, peeling paint, and insect damage
- **Fiber cement** — inspect for cracking at nail holes and joints
- **Brick veneer** — note step cracks, efflorescence, and weep hole blockage
- **Stucco** — check for cracking patterns and delamination

## Grading and Drainage

- Minimum 6 inches of foundation visible above grade
- Positive slope away from the building (6" drop in the first 10 feet)
- Downspout extensions discharging 4–6 feet from the foundation

:::warning
Negative grading is one of the most common causes of basement water intrusion. Always document grade conditions at all sides of the building.
:::
