# HVAC Systems Overview

HVAC inspection covers heating equipment, cooling equipment, distribution systems (ductwork or piping), thermostats, and air filtration. These systems directly affect occupant comfort, energy costs, and indoor air quality.

## Heating Systems

| Type | Fuel | Key Inspection Points |
|------|------|----------------------|
| Forced-air furnace | Gas, oil, electric | Heat exchanger, burner flame, flue venting |
| Boiler (hydronic) | Gas, oil | Pressure/temperature relief, expansion tank, circulator pump |
| Heat pump | Electric | Reversing valve, defrost cycle, auxiliary heat |
| Baseboard electric | Electric | Clearance from combustibles, thermostat function |

:::danger
A cracked heat exchanger on a gas furnace can leak carbon monoxide into the living space. Look for visible cracks, flame rollout marks, and soot at supply registers.
:::

## Cooling Systems

- **Condenser unit** — check for level placement, clean coils, and adequate clearance (12–24 inches on all sides)
- **Refrigerant lines** — insulation on the suction line should be intact; no oil stains at connections
- **Temperature split** — measure supply and return air temperatures; expect a 15–20°F differential in cooling mode
- **Condensate drainage** — verify the drain line is clear and routed to an appropriate termination point

## Ductwork and Filtration

- **Duct condition** — look for disconnected sections, crushed flex duct, and excessive length
- **Air sealing** — gaps at connections waste energy and pull in unconditioned air
- **Filter condition** — note size, type, and whether it was dirty or missing at time of inspection
- **Return air** — every level of the home should have adequate return air pathways

:::tip
Always remove the filter and photograph it. A clogged filter is the single most common HVAC maintenance failure you will document.
:::

## Common Defects

- **Dirty or missing filters** — restricts airflow and accelerates equipment wear
- **Disconnected ductwork** — especially in attics and crawl spaces
- **Improper flue venting** — single-wall vent pipe too close to combustibles
- **Aging equipment** — furnaces and AC units past 15–20 years warrant budgeting for replacement
