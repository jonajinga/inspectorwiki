# Interior & Finishes Overview

Interior inspection covers walls, ceilings, floors, doors, windows, and stairways. While often considered cosmetic, interior conditions frequently indicate structural, moisture, or mechanical system issues.

## Walls and Ceilings

- **Cracks** — diagonal cracks at corners of doors and windows suggest settlement
- **Water stains** — ceiling stains may indicate roof leaks, plumbing leaks, or condensation
- **Bulging or bowing** — may indicate structural movement or moisture damage

## Floors

- Noticeable slope or bounce may indicate structural issues (joist damage, settlement)
- Check for moisture damage near kitchens, bathrooms, and exterior doors

## Windows and Doors

- Test operation of a representative number of windows
- Check for broken seals (foggy double-pane glass)
- Verify egress windows in bedrooms meet size requirements

:::tip
A marble or ball placed on the floor is a quick way to identify slope, but a digital level provides more accurate and reportable measurements.
:::
