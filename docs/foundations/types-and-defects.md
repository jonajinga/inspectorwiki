# Foundation Types and Common Defects

The foundation is the most critical structural component of any building. Understanding foundation types and their failure modes is essential for every property inspector.

## Common Foundation Types

### Poured Concrete

The most common type in modern residential construction. Poured concrete walls offer good strength and water resistance when properly constructed. Look for:

- Vertical cracks (often shrinkage — note width and any displacement)
- Horizontal cracks (potential lateral pressure — always report)
- Honeycomb voids from poor consolidation
- Cold joints where pours didn't bond

### Concrete Block (CMU)

Concrete masonry unit foundations are common in older homes and certain regions. They're more susceptible to water infiltration and lateral failure than poured walls.

:::warning
Stair-step cracking in block foundations often indicates differential settlement. Always measure and document the crack width and any vertical displacement.
:::

### Stone and Rubble

Found in pre-1940s homes. These foundations rely on mortar and gravity. Deteriorated mortar joints, bowing walls, and water infiltration are common findings.

## Inspection Approach

1. **Walk the exterior perimeter** — look for visible cracks, displacement, and grading issues
2. **Check interior basement/crawlspace walls** — note any cracks, efflorescence, or water staining
3. **Check floors for level** — a marble test or digital level can reveal settlement
4. **Document everything** — photograph cracks with a reference scale

## Reporting Standards

Per ASHI Standard of Practice, inspectors must report on the foundation's visible condition. You are not required to determine the cause of defects, but you should recommend further evaluation by a structural engineer when significant cracking, displacement, or bowing is present.
