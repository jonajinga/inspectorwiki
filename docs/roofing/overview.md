# Roofing Systems Overview

The roof is one of the most critical and most commonly under-inspected systems in any building. A thorough roof inspection evaluates covering materials, flashing, drainage, penetrations, and the structure beneath.

## Common Roofing Materials

| Material | Expected Lifespan | Key Concerns |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Asphalt (3-tab) | 15–20 years | Curling, granule loss, wind damage |
| Architectural shingles | 25–30 years | Lifted tabs, improper nailing |
| Metal (standing seam) | 40–60 years | Fastener corrosion, panel separation |
| Tile (clay/concrete) | 50–100 years | Cracked tiles, underlayment failure |
| Flat/low-slope (TPO, EPDM) | 20–30 years | Ponding, seam failure, punctures |

## Five Things New Inspectors Miss

1. **Improper kick-out flashing** at roof-to-wall intersections
2. **Lifted or cupped shingles** indicating wind or moisture damage
3. **Inadequate attic ventilation** accelerating shingle aging
4. **Failed pipe boot flashing** (rubber cracks after 10–15 years)
5. **Gutter slope and attachment** issues affecting drainage

:::tip
Bring binoculars on every inspection. You'll catch lifted flashing and cracked boots from the ground that you'd miss with the naked eye.
:::

## Attic and Ventilation

The roof's condition is inseparable from the attic. Always check:

- Soffit-to-ridge airflow path
- Signs of condensation or frost on sheathing
- Daylight visible through the roof deck
- Proper bathroom/kitchen exhaust venting to exterior (not into attic)
