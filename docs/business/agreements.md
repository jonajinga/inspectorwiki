# Agreements

A well-drafted pre-inspection agreement protects both you and your client. It defines the scope of the inspection, sets expectations, and limits liability.

## Key Components

Every pre-inspection agreement should include:

- **Scope of inspection** — reference your applicable standard of practice (ASHI, InterNACHI, or state SoP)
- **Limitations** — what is excluded (concealed areas, cosmetic defects, code compliance)
- **Liability cap** — typically limited to the fee paid for the inspection
- **Dispute resolution** — mediation or arbitration clause
- **Client acknowledgment** — signature confirming understanding of scope and limitations

## E-Signatures

Use electronic signature tools integrated with your inspection software. Most platforms (Spectora, HomeGauge, Palm-Tech) include built-in agreement signing during the booking process.

:::danger
Never perform an inspection without a signed agreement. Operating without one exposes you to unlimited liability and makes insurance claims significantly more difficult.
:::

## Attorney Review

Have a local attorney familiar with your state's inspection laws review your agreement annually. State regulations change, and your agreement must stay current.
