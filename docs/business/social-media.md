# Social Media

Social media builds awareness and credibility, but it's rarely a direct lead source for inspectors. Focus on platforms where your referral partners (agents) and potential clients spend time.

## Platform Priority

1. **Facebook** — local community groups, agent networking, before/after photos
2. **Instagram** — visual defect documentation, project highlights, reels
3. **LinkedIn** — professional networking, agent connections, industry content
4. **YouTube** — educational videos, walkthroughs, defect explanations
5. **TikTok** — short inspection clips, surprisingly effective for brand awareness

## Content Ideas

- Defect of the week (with educational explanation)
- Inspection day behind-the-scenes
- Seasonal maintenance tips for homeowners
- "What your inspector looks at" explainer series
- Team introductions and credential highlights

## Posting Frequency

Consistency matters more than volume. Two to three quality posts per week is sustainable and effective. Batch-create content during inspections by photographing interesting findings (with client permission).
