# Logo Design

A strong logo establishes credibility before a client ever speaks with you. For inspection companies, the logo appears on reports, business cards, vehicle wraps, and websites — it needs to work at every size.

## Design Principles

Keep it simple. The best inspection logos are legible at 1 inch wide and look clean in black and white. Avoid clip art houses or magnifying glasses that every competitor uses. A custom wordmark or abstract mark will age better and distinguish your brand.

Choose two colors maximum for the primary logo. Dark blues, greens, and grays convey trust and professionalism. Reserve accent colors for calls to action on your website and marketing materials.

:::tip
Request your logo in SVG, PNG (transparent background), and single-color versions. You will need all three for different applications.
:::

## Where to Get a Logo

- **Freelance designers** — Platforms like 99designs or Fiverr offer budget options ($50–$500).
- **Local design agencies** — Higher cost ($500–$2,000+) but typically includes a full brand guide with typography, color codes, and usage rules.
- **DIY tools** — Canva or Looka can produce serviceable logos if budget is extremely tight, but they lack originality.

:::warning
Avoid free logo generators that retain licensing rights. Always confirm you own full commercial rights to your final logo files.
:::
