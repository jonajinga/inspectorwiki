# Standards of Practice Overview

Standards of Practice (SoP) define the scope, limitations, and requirements of a property inspection. They protect both inspectors and clients by setting clear expectations.

## Major Standards

- **ASHI SoP** — The American Society of Home Inspectors standard is widely adopted and referenced in many state licensing laws
- **InterNACHI SoP** — The International Association of Certified Home Inspectors standard, often considered more detailed
- **State-specific standards** — Many states have their own SoP codified in licensing regulations

## Key Principles

All major standards share common principles:

1. Inspections are **visual, non-invasive** examinations of readily accessible systems
2. Inspectors are **not required to determine causes** of defects — only to report observable conditions
3. Inspectors must **recommend further evaluation** when conditions exceed the scope of a general inspection
4. Certain items are **excluded** — concealed components, cosmetic defects, code compliance verification
