# Electrical Systems Overview

Electrical inspection covers the service entrance, main panel, sub-panels, branch circuits, outlets, switches, and grounding. Electrical defects are among the most safety-critical findings an inspector can report.

## Service Entrance

Inspect the service drop (overhead) or lateral (underground):

- **Weatherhead condition** — proper drip loops, secure attachment
- **Service entrance cables** — no damage, proper clearance from ground and windows
- **Meter base** — sealed, no signs of tampering or corrosion

## Main Panel

- **Panel brand** — identify recalled brands (Federal Pacific, Zinsco, Challenger)
- **Breaker condition** — look for scorch marks, double-tapped breakers, improper sizing
- **Grounding and bonding** — verify ground rod, water pipe bond, neutral-ground bond at main only
- **Wire sizing** — match wire gauge to breaker amperage

:::danger
Federal Pacific Stab-Lok panels have a well-documented failure rate. Always recommend evaluation by a licensed electrician when one is identified.
:::

## Branch Circuits

- Test GFCI protection in kitchens, bathrooms, garages, exteriors, and basements
- Check for open grounds, reversed polarity, and bootleg grounds
- Verify AFCI protection where required by current code
- Note any aluminum branch wiring (pre-1972 homes)

## Common Defects

- **Double-tapped breakers** — two wires on a single-pole breaker not rated for it
- **Missing knockouts** — open holes in the panel enclosure
- **Improper splices** — wire connections outside of junction boxes
- **Undersized wiring** — 14-gauge wire on 20-amp circuits
