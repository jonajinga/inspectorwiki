# Insulation & Ventilation Overview

Proper insulation and ventilation work together to maintain energy efficiency, prevent moisture problems, and extend the life of the building envelope.

## Insulation Types

| Type | R-Value per Inch | Common Locations |
|------|------------------|------------------|
| Fiberglass batts | R-3.1 to R-3.7 | Attics, walls, crawlspaces |
| Blown cellulose | R-3.2 to R-3.8 | Attics, enclosed walls |
| Spray foam (closed) | R-6.0 to R-7.0 | Rim joists, crawlspaces, walls |
| Rigid foam board | R-3.8 to R-6.5 | Foundations, exterior walls |

## Attic Ventilation

Balanced ventilation requires both intake (soffit) and exhaust (ridge, gable, or roof vents). Never mix active and passive exhaust systems on the same roof — it short-circuits airflow.

:::warning
Insulation covering soffit vents blocks intake airflow and leads to moisture buildup, ice dams, and premature shingle failure.
:::
