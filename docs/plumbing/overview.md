# Plumbing Systems Overview

Plumbing inspection covers the water supply system, drain/waste/vent (DWV) system, water heating equipment, and all visible fixtures. Plumbing defects can cause significant water damage, mold growth, and health hazards if left unaddressed.

## Supply System

The supply system delivers pressurized water from the municipal main or private well to fixtures throughout the home:

- **Main shutoff valve** — locate and verify operability (gate valves may seize over time)
- **Pipe materials** — copper, PEX, CPVC, or galvanized steel (note any polybutylene, which is failure-prone)
- **Water pressure** — test at hose bibs; normal range is 40–80 psi
- **Cross-connections** — identify any unprotected connections that could allow backflow

:::warning
Polybutylene (PB) supply piping, common in homes built 1978–1995, is prone to sudden failure at fittings and connections. Always note its presence and recommend further evaluation.
:::

## Drain, Waste, and Vent (DWV)

- **Drain function** — run water at every fixture and observe drainage speed
- **Vent terminations** — verify vent pipes extend through the roof and are not obstructed
- **Pipe materials** — cast iron, ABS, PVC, or older galvanized (check for corrosion and scaling)
- **S-traps** — identify improper trap configurations that can siphon and allow sewer gas entry

## Water Heaters

- **Type and age** — decode the serial number to determine manufacture date
- **Temperature and pressure relief (TPR) valve** — verify presence and proper discharge piping to within 6 inches of the floor
- **Combustion air** — gas units need adequate ventilation in their enclosure
- **Flue draft** — check for proper draft and no backdrafting on atmospheric-vent units

## Common Defects

- **Leaking supply valves** — especially angle stops under sinks and toilets
- **Slow drains** — may indicate partial blockage or improper slope
- **Missing TPR discharge pipe** — a safety hazard on every water heater
- **Galvanized drain pipes** — interior corrosion restricts flow over time
