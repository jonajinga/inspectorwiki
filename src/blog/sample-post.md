title: "5 Things Every New Inspector Misses on Their First Roof"
description: "Common roof defects that new inspectors overlook, and how to train your eye to catch them every time."
date: 2026-04-10
---

Starting out in property inspection means developing an eye for defects that only comes with experience. The roof is one of the most critical — and most commonly under-inspected — systems in any building.

## 1. Improper Kick-Out Flashing

Where a roof slope meets a sidewall, water needs to be directed away from the wall. Without kick-out flashing, water runs behind the siding and causes hidden rot. Look for staining, bubbling paint, or missing flashing at every roof-to-wall intersection.

## 2. Lifted or Cupped Shingles

Wind damage isn't always obvious. Run your eye along each course and look for shingles that sit higher than their neighbors. Cupping indicates moisture damage from below — often a ventilation issue.

## 3. Inadequate Attic Ventilation

The roof's condition is inseparable from what's happening in the attic. Check for proper soffit-to-ridge airflow. Blocked soffit vents or missing ridge vents accelerate shingle aging from the underside.

## 4. Flashing at Penetrations

Every pipe boot, chimney, and skylight needs intact flashing. Rubber pipe boots crack after 10–15 years. Chimney step flashing that's been caulked over (rather than properly counter-flashed) is a red flag.

## 5. Gutter Condition and Slope

Gutters that pull away from the fascia, slope the wrong direction, or dump water at the foundation aren't just a gutter problem — they're a roof drainage problem. Always check the full water path from ridge to grade.

{% callout "tip" %}
Bring binoculars on every inspection. You'll catch lifted flashing and cracked boots from the ground that you'd miss with the naked eye.
{% endcallout %}
